Tonight, Even the Sky Lined Up

Moon · Venus · Jupiter · Mercury — low in the western sky after sunset, tonight only

If you look west just after sunset tonight, you'll find the Moon sitting close to Venus, Jupiter, and Mercury — all of them lined up in a soft diagonal across the dusk. Astronomers have a name for it. I have a simpler one: the universe finally agreeing with me.

I picked today to give you this, zilmil, because for once, even the sky is doing the thing you do to me every day — lining everything up, and making it impossible to look away.

a name only I use

Why I call you Zilmil ✦

Zilmil isn't really a word. It's the sound the sky makes when the stars are trying to tell you something — that flicker, that wink, that little blink-and-it's-gone shimmer right before midnight.

I started calling you that somewhere between a random Tuesday and a parrot story I'm still not over telling. Because somehow, without asking permission, you switched on a light in my life I didn't even know had gone out.

So now, every time the sky does that twinkly little thing it does — I think of you. Not by the name everyone else uses. By the one that's only ours.

Our Story, So Far

chapter one

Stranger in Red

The first time I saw you, you were wearing red, standing somewhere near a half-finished canvas at the office painting event — and I remember thinking, wow, what a girl, and immediately filing you under "stranger, do not get attached."

We weren't friends yet. We were just two people in the same room — except you, for some reason, sat through my entire parrot story without once getting up to leave. That should have been my first clue that you were trouble. The good kind.

Then the universe did its thing — the office seats got reshuffled, and somehow I landed on seat 414, right next to you. Coincidence, probably. Destiny, I prefer to believe.

chapter two

The Christmas That Came Early

Then came the CSR event — the one right before Christmas — and somehow Christmas decided to show up early that year. We rode the bus together, ate ice cream like it was a serious team-building exercise, and walked around like we had nowhere better to be. We didn't.

And on the auto ride back — you hugged me. The first time. I don't remember everything we talked about that day. I remember exactly how that hug felt.

chapter three

Radha, Krishna, and a Stall

Navratri turned you into Radha and me into your slightly less graceful Krishna, and we still somehow pulled off a "Sami Sami" performance that nobody asked for but everybody enjoyed.

Then Diwali handed me the bumper offer of the year — a few hours helping you run your very first stall, both of us pretending we knew what we were doing. There was a song playing somewhere in the background that night, something about catching someone stealing glances when they think no one's looking. I didn't have to imagine it. I was doing exactly that.

You're the light in rooms that don't deserve you, and the moon that still bothers to show up on the cloudy nights. Whether you need me there or not — your happy place will always have my name quietly written somewhere in the corner of it.
